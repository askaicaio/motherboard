# GWBD Operations: Faith-driven plumbing manufacturer on an acquisition tear

**GWBD Operations, Inc. is a privately held, family-owned holding company in Grandview, Missouri that operates three American plumbing and building products brands — anchored by PRIER Products, a 145-year-old brass hydrant manufacturer.** The company is led by CEO and owner Joe Poskin (since 1994), has completed three acquisitions since 2007, and is actively seeking more. GWBD's consolidated revenue likely falls in the **$50M–$80M range** (estimated), with roughly 150–250 employees across facilities in Missouri and Kentucky. The name "GWBD" almost certainly stands for **"God's Will Be Done,"** consistent with the company's overtly faith-based culture and commitment to donating at least 10% of profits to charity.

---

## What GWBD makes and how the brands fit together

GWBD Operations functions as a holding company umbrella over three distinct subsidiaries:

**PRIER Products** (est. 1881) is the flagship — a brass plumbing manufacturer specializing in residential and commercial hydrants (wall hydrants, yard hydrants, hose bibs), freezeless faucets, angle sill faucets, lead-free plumbing brass, drainage products, and specialty valves. Notable innovations include the **P-118 TrueTemp** hot/cold outdoor hydrant, the world's first freezeless outdoor shower, and a freezeless roof hydrant. PRIER uses **red brass** (higher copper content, more corrosion-resistant) rather than the yellow brass common among competitors, and holds multiple patents. Products are distributed through Ferguson, WinSupply, Home Depot, Lowe's, Menard's, and Ace Hardware across North America.

**Stern-Williams Products** (est. 1962, Carterville, MO) manufactures terrazzo service sinks, drinking fountains, and shower towers. **FIAT Products** (est. 1919, Somerset, KY) produces mop basins, laundry tubs, and acrylic/terrazzo shower modules. Together, the three brands give GWBD a complementary portfolio spanning **plumbing fixtures, commercial washroom products, and institutional building products** — all within NAICS 332 (Fabricated Metal Product Manufacturing) and adjacent segments.

## Poskin family ownership and an acquisition-led growth strategy

Joe Poskin has served as **CEO and owner since 1994**. His son **Brady Poskin** (London Business School MSc) serves as Chief Strategy Officer, driving "growth and operational excellence across GWBD's multi-brand portfolio, with a strong focus on expansion through acquisition." **Nick Manning** serves as President of GWBD Operations, and **Bryce Williams** is CFO. The company is **not PE-backed** — it is a family-controlled business, though it received a **$7.5M structured debt investment** from Advantage Capital in 2022 to finance the FIAT Products acquisition under Kentucky's New Markets Development Program.

GWBD's acquisition history tells a clear roll-up story:

- **2007**: Acquired the plumbing brass division of Mansfield Plumbing Products, expanding PRIER's residential hydrant line
- **December 2020**: Acquired Stern-Williams Products (terrazzo sinks, drinking fountains)
- **March 2022**: Acquired FIAT Products (mop basins, shower modules), transitioning it from foreign to American ownership

The company is **listed on Axial** (a lower-middle-market M&A platform) and is actively seeking further acquisitions in "plumbing, specialty manufacturing, and related building product niches." Nick Manning has stated that GWBD's differentiator is how it "reassesses and molds acquired businesses to align with PRIER's vision and opportunities." The aggressive sales team expansion in 2024–2025 — hiring three new regional/national sales managers including a dedicated **National Sales Manager for Canada** — signals an active growth phase.

## 145 years of American brass manufacturing

PRIER was founded in **1881** by Anton Prier, a German immigrant who started a brass foundry in Kansas City manufacturing coach lanterns, plaques, and household goods. The company pivoted to defense manufacturing during WWII (specialty products for the U.S. Navy) and again during the Korean War. It found its permanent niche in heavy brass plumbing parts in the post-Korea era, introducing its **first freezeless faucet in 1946**. Key modern milestones include the 2003 launch of a hot/cold wall hydrant, the 2007 Mansfield acquisition, the 2021 launch of several innovative products (freezeless outdoor shower, freezeless roof hydrant, irrigation valve), and a **2021 Sustainability Award** from the Kansas City Industrial Council. All products remain **100% manufactured in the U.S.** at the Grandview, MO campus.

## Competitors range from niche rivals to billion-dollar water companies

PRIER's competitive landscape splits into two tiers. The most **direct competitors** are similarly sized, private hydrant manufacturers:

- **Woodford Manufacturing** (est. 1929, Colorado Springs, CO) — the closest head-to-head rival across residential freezeless faucets, yard hydrants, commercial wall hydrants, and roof hydrants
- **Merrill Manufacturing** (Storm Lake, IA) — strong in frost-proof yard hydrants with the AnyFlow® and AnyTemp® heated lines
- **Simmons Manufacturing** (est. 1957, McDonough, GA) — lead-free yard hydrants distributed nationally
- **Arrowhead Brass** (Denver area) and **Clayton Mark** (Milwaukee area) — competing in retail outdoor faucet/hydrant channels

The broader competitive tier includes publicly traded water solutions giants: **Watts Water Technologies** (NYSE: WTS, **$1.81B revenue**) and **Zurn Elkay Water Solutions** (NYSE: ZWS), both of which compete in commercial plumbing but operate at vastly different scales. Private-label brands like **Everbilt** (Home Depot) also compete at retail.

## Industry headwinds center on labor, materials, and housing

PRIER's manufacturing segment faces several converging pressures. The **skilled labor shortage** is perhaps the most acute: for every five plumbers retiring, only one enters an apprenticeship, and the average plumber is over 40. GWBD has responded by partnering with Missouri One Start and Metropolitan Community College for workforce development. **Material costs** remain elevated for brass, copper, steel, and aluminum — PRIER's core inputs. Reinstated Section 232 tariffs in early 2025 on a wider range of metal products add further input cost pressure, though they also raise barriers for foreign competitors. The **housing market** remains soft due to high mortgage rates, though the "lock-in effect" (57% of homeowners hold sub-4% mortgages) drives remodeling and repair demand — a net positive for replacement parts. Commercial construction in **data centers, healthcare, and education** remains a bright spot. Broader tailwinds include the **reshoring trend** (manufacturing up 21% in 2024 per FMI) and tightening lead-free and water efficiency regulations, both of which favor PRIER's domestic, compliant manufacturing.

## Larger competitors are investing heavily in AI; PRIER's tier is not

The AI adoption gap in this industry tracks closely with company size. **Watts Water Technologies** is aggressively investing — actively hiring Senior AI Developers for an Intelligent Process Automation team, launching the **Nexa** IoT water management platform with real-time monitoring and predictive analytics, and migrating to Oracle Cloud with NTT DATA for process automation. **Zurn Elkay** has pursued extensive digital transformation under CIO Mike Schmidt, patenting its **plumbSMART™** connected water device management platform and investing in IIoT-connected products, predictive maintenance, and energy optimization. However, **no evidence exists** of PRIER's direct competitors (Woodford, Merrill, Simmons, Murdock) adopting AI or digital transformation — these remain traditional manufacturers. Industry-wide, 90% of manufacturers report adopting "some form" of AI as of 2025, but this is heavily skewed toward larger firms.

## What makes GWBD distinctive in its niche

GWBD's competitive moat rests on several reinforcing differentiators. **100% U.S. manufacturing** from its Grandview campus enables an **8-hour order shipping guarantee** with no minimums, no broken carton charges, and no return fees — a service model unusual in the industry. The use of **red brass** and heavy cast patterns positions products as premium durability plays. **Replacement parts available going back 60+ years** creates deep contractor loyalty. Engineering innovations have reduced customer SKU requirements by over 50%, simplifying inventory management for distributors.

On sustainability, PRIER stands out: **solar panels** on all buildings (selling excess energy back to the grid), **330,000+ kWh saved annually**, 50% recycled raw material inputs, 95% of products fully recyclable, and a circular economy model where brass scrap returns to suppliers for remelting. The company's faith-based mission — donating **at least 10% of annual profits** through the Greater Kansas City Community Foundation — is a genuine cultural differentiator that shapes hiring, partnerships, and brand identity.

## Conclusion

GWBD Operations represents a classic **lower-middle-market roll-up** in niche building products — a family-owned platform acquiring complementary brands, centralizing operational culture, and growing through distribution expansion. The company's strengths for AI consulting purposes are notable: it has clear operational complexity across three brands and multiple facilities, a strong manufacturing base that could benefit from predictive maintenance and quality vision systems, supply chain optimization opportunities across brass/copper procurement, and a growing sales organization that could leverage AI-powered demand forecasting. The fact that direct competitors remain technology-traditional while larger players like Watts and Zurn are investing heavily in AI creates both a **competitive risk** (falling behind on smart/connected products) and an **opportunity** (leapfrogging peers through targeted automation and analytics). GWBD's acquisition-driven growth model also creates recurring integration challenges — standardizing processes, ERP systems, and data infrastructure across acquired companies — where AI consulting could deliver significant value.
