# Académie Lafayette: Missouri's top-performing French immersion charter network

Académie Lafayette is a **501(c)(3) public charter school network** in Kansas City, Missouri, founded in 1999 as the state's first charter school. It operates the **largest public French immersion program in the United States**, serving approximately **1,352 students across three campuses** from Pre-K through 12th grade. The school earned the **highest Annual Performance Report score in Missouri (99.7%)** in 2023–24 out of 550+ districts and charter schools, and recently secured **$13.6 million in bond funding** through a historic partnership with Kansas City Public Schools. With $21.4 million in annual revenue and a 96% Charity Navigator rating, the school is financially healthy but faces real pressure from a $1.7 million state funding clawback and chronic bilingual teacher shortages.

---

## From 250 students to Missouri's top-ranked district in 25 years

Académie Lafayette traces its origins to the collapse of the Kansas City, Missouri School District's immersion magnet program following a 1995 court order. Parents from those immersion sites petitioned for a charter in late 1998, and the school opened in August 1999 with **250 students in grades K–8** in a classroom wing of Temple B'nai Jehudah. Originally named "The Kansas City Foreign Language Charter School," it was renamed Académie Lafayette in 2002 to honor the Marquis de Lafayette's role in the American Revolution.

The organization is structured as a **tax-exempt 501(c)(3) nonprofit** (EIN: 43-1824141) governed by an independent Board of Directors comprising both appointed members (three-year renewable terms) and parent-elected members (three-year terms). **Saint Louis University** has served as the charter authorizer since 2022, replacing the University of Central Missouri, which ended all charter sponsorships that year. The current charter runs through 2029.

The school's mission statement reads: *"To educate the children of Kansas City to become globally minded, locally active, critical thinkers through immersion in world languages and cultures."* Students must reside within Kansas City Public Schools district boundaries to enroll.

## A $21.4 million operation with 219% revenue growth over 13 years

Académie Lafayette's financial trajectory reflects steady growth driven overwhelmingly by per-pupil state and local charter funding. The most recent IRS Form 990 filing (FY2024, ending June 2024) shows **total revenue of $21,415,838**, **total expenses of $19,697,112**, and **net assets of $10,629,041**. Revenue has grown from $6.7 million in FY2011 to $21.4 million in FY2024—a **219% increase** over 13 years. The school has posted positive net income every year except FY2022 (a small $232,205 loss), and net assets have grown nearly **eightfold** from $1.3 million to $10.6 million in the same period.

**Program services revenue** (state and local per-pupil allocations) accounts for **96.1% of total revenue** ($20.6 million in FY2024), with contributions and grants comprising just 2.8% ($608,218). Salaries and wages represent approximately **51% of total expenses** ($10 million in FY2024). Charity Navigator awards the school a **96% score and four-star rating** based on accountability and finance metrics.

Per-pupil spending stands at approximately **$11,032** based on NCES fiscal data. Missouri's State Adequacy Target for per-pupil funding rose to **$6,760 per weighted average daily attendance** in 2025, a 10% jump from the prior year. Under Missouri law (160.415 RSMo), charter schools receive the same per-pupil local aid as their resident district (KCPS). However, in December 2024, DESE notified all Kansas City charter schools of an **unexpected $11.8 million collective funding clawback**, with Académie Lafayette's share at **$1.7 million** to be repaid over four months—a significant budget shock caused by the expiration of pandemic-era attendance provisions.

Superintendent Elimane Mbengue received **$159,136 in compensation** plus $36,584 in benefits in FY2024. Director of Finance Steven Livingston earned $119,840. All board directors serve without compensation.

## 1,352 students, a 99.7% state performance score, and high demand

Enrollment stands at approximately **1,352 students** across all campuses (per Niche and U.S. News, 2023–24 data). The school accepts **210 kindergarteners annually** through a lottery-based system administered via **SchoolAppKC**, a unified Kansas City charter school application platform. Students can enter without French at Pre-K, kindergarten, 1st grade, and 9th–12th grade; grades 2–8 require French fluency. Sibling priority exists in the lottery, and demand consistently exceeds available seats, generating waitlists.

The student-teacher ratio is approximately **21:1** according to Niche, though NCES-derived figures from SchoolDigger suggest lower ratios of **12.5–15.9:1** depending on campus and how part-time staff are counted. The student body is **57.4% white, 18.1% Black, 13.2% multiracial, and 9.8% Hispanic**, with **21.7% qualifying as economically disadvantaged**.

Academic performance is the school's strongest selling point. The **2023–24 Missouri Annual Performance Report score of 99.7%** was the highest of any district or charter school in the state. On Missouri Assessment Program (MAP) tests, the district averages **49% math proficiency and 63% reading proficiency**, both significantly above the state averages of 41.1% and 43% respectively. The Armour middle school performs particularly well at **58% math and 72% reading**. SchoolDigger historically ranked Académie Lafayette **#1 out of 479 Missouri school districts** in 2016–17.

Ratings across major platforms confirm strong performance:

- **GreatSchools**: Oak 9/10, Armour 8/10, Cherry 6/10
- **Niche**: Armour rated A; #2 Best Charter High School in Missouri; #10 Best Public High School in Missouri
- **SchoolDigger**: Armour Middle School ranked 13th out of 459 Missouri middle schools (97th percentile)

The school also delivers **IB Middle Years Programme** (grades 6–8) and **IB Diploma Programme** (authorized May 2022 for grades 11–12), adding a third language (Mandarin or Spanish) starting in 6th grade. Students participate in international travel to France, Senegal, Costa Rica, and China, with 7th graders spending 2.5 weeks with host families in France.

## Leadership under Superintendent Mbengue and a diverse staff from 23 countries

**Elimane Mbengue** serves as Superintendent (title upgraded from Head of School), having led the school since 2012. Born in Senegal and educated at the Ecole Normale Supérieure of Dakar, Mbengue holds a Certificate of Business Management from Portland State University and completed the National Institute for School Leadership Executive Development Program. The French government awarded him the **Chevalier dans l'Ordre des Palmes Académiques** in 2018, and Ingram's Magazine named him a **2024 Icon of Education**. He serves on the Advisory Board of Avila University's Strategic AI Program.

**Board President Michele Markham** (appointed 2020) is owner of EAG Advertising & Marketing. **Vice President Lauren Allen, Esq.** (parent-elected 2022) is General Counsel for the Housing Authority of Kansas City. **Secretary Olivier Griot** (appointed 2024) chairs the Finance Committee and holds an MBA from Wharton.

Building leadership includes **Heather Royce** (Cherry Campus Principal since 2011), **Jean-Claude Diatta** (Oak Campus Vice Principal, with the school since 2000), **Dr. Nadia Paul** (High School Principal, PhD in French Literature from CU Boulder), and **Dorothée Werner** (educated at the Sorbonne). Central office leadership includes Director of Finance **Steven Livingston**, Director of Admissions **Whitney Molloy**, Director of Development **Alyson Stewart**, and Director of HR **Laura Richards**.

The school employs an estimated **100–150 total staff** (LinkedIn reports 51–200 employees; NCES cites 89.58 FTE). Staff hail from **over 23 different countries**. Bilingual teacher recruitment remains a persistent challenge: job postings explicitly state the school **cannot provide work visas**, the careers page advertises a **relocation stipend**, and the school uses international teaching interns and native French speakers in kindergarten classrooms to supplement staffing. The 2023–2028 strategic plan identifies recruiting, supporting, and retaining effective educators as a core goal. Current openings include approximately 8–9 positions spanning French teachers, special education staff, a Computer & Technology Teacher, school nurses, and bus drivers.

## $13.6 million bond, IB expansion, and a 2,000-student growth target

The school's most significant recent development is the **$474 million KCPS General Obligation Bond**, which passed on April 8, 2025, with **85% voter approval**—the first KCPS bond in nearly 60 years. Académie Lafayette was allocated **$13.6 million** for facility improvements in what Superintendent Mbengue described as an unprecedented national model of a traditional district partnering with charter schools. Completed projects as of summer 2025 include roof replacements and athletic field improvements at Cherry and Oak campuses, SmartBoard installations at Armour and Oak, and Cherry office renovations. Pending projects include an **Armour campus gymnasium and auditorium** (summer/fall 2026), Oak campus HVAC and window replacements, and Cherry auditorium HVAC upgrades.

The facility needs are acute. The Armour campus currently lacks a gym and auditorium—students attend PE in a room with a ceiling too low for basketball hoops and window AC units that create noise interference for language instruction. These conditions reportedly drive some high school students to transfer to schools with better athletic facilities.

The **2023–2028 Strategic Plan, titled "Raising the Bar: Ensuring a High-Quality Education for Every Student,"** establishes four pillars: aligning student demographics with KCPS boundaries (targeting 27% low-SES enrollment and 95% retention across all demographics), fostering inclusive school culture, achieving academic excellence for all students, and building an effective educator workforce. The high school growth plan, funded by a **$1.3 million federal grant** and a **$1.5 million SchoolSmartKC grant**, targets full K–12 enrollment exceeding 2,000 students.

Past capital campaigns include the **$3.5 million "Give to Grow" campaign** (completed 2014, building a six-classroom wing at Cherry) and **$3.2 million raised in 2017** from philanthropic organizations plus $1.8 million through New Markets Tax Credits for the Armour building acquisition and renovation. The annual **"Evening in Paris"** gala serves as the signature fundraising event each May.

## Operating within a growing but constrained French immersion landscape

Académie Lafayette exists at the intersection of several significant education trends. The U.S. dual language immersion market has expanded rapidly to **3,600–4,900+ programs across 44 states**, with the market exceeding **$1.5 billion in 2023** and growing at approximately 25% CAGR. French represents roughly **5% of all DLI programs** (approximately 173–180 programs in 30+ states), with the French Embassy's network serving more than **33,000 students in 180 schools**. However, growth is constrained by teacher supply: **47 out of 50 states** report severe language teacher shortages, and school administrators cite inability to recruit qualified teachers as the most common reason for closing French programs.

Missouri's charter school funding landscape presents both opportunity and risk. Charter schools operate under a formula granting per-pupil local aid equivalent to their resident district, but facilities funding remains a chronic gap since charters lack taxing authority or bonding capacity. The December 2024 clawback of $11.8 million from KC charters exposed the fragility of the funding mechanism. A **Missouri School Funding Modernization Task Force** is developing recommendations for a new K–12 formula, due by December 2026, while SB 727 (2024) expanded charter authorization to Boone County—a move facing legal challenge.

AI adoption in K–12 education is accelerating rapidly. RAND Corporation's 2025 survey found **53% of teachers and 54% of students** now use AI for school—increases of more than 15 percentage points in just one to two years. The global AI in K–12 market reached **$390.8 million in 2024** and is projected to reach **$7.95 billion by 2033** at 38.1% CAGR. The broader K-12 EdTech market stands at approximately **$18.5 billion** and is projected to reach $132.4 billion by 2032. Superintendent Mbengue's appointment to the Advisory Board of Avila University's Strategic AI Program signals the school's awareness of this trend, though no AI-specific tools were identified in the school's current technology stack.

## French government, KCPS, and community organizations anchor the partnership network

Académie Lafayette maintains partnerships spanning French cultural institutions, local organizations, and the international education community. The **French Consulate General** recognized the school when Consul General Guillaume Lacroix visited in 2018, awarding Mbengue the Palmes Académiques and calling the school "a shining star of education in Kansas City" and "an immense asset for France and more broadly to Francophonie."

**Saint Louis University** serves as charter sponsor, providing oversight through its Office of School and Community Partnerships—a particularly strategic relationship since, as administrators noted, SLU's university status enables support with visa processes for international French-speaking teachers. **SchoolSmartKC** provided a $1.5 million grant for the high school launch. The **International Baccalaureate Organization** authorized the school for both MYP and Diploma Programme. **Upper Room KC** operates after-school programming at Oak and Cherry campuses, providing homework assistance, enrichment activities, and French lessons.

The KCPS bond partnership represents perhaps the most significant institutional relationship—an unprecedented arrangement where a traditional school district shares general obligation bond funding with charter schools. The school is also a member of the **Diverse Charter Schools Coalition** and the **Missouri Charter Public School Association**, the latter featuring Académie Lafayette prominently in statewide advocacy efforts.

## Conclusion

Académie Lafayette has evolved from a 250-student startup in a synagogue classroom wing into a **1,352-student, $21.4 million K–12 operation** that holds Missouri's highest performance score. Its financial position is strong—net assets have grown eightfold since 2011 and the Charity Navigator rating sits at 96%—but the December 2024 funding clawback of $1.7 million and the structural underfunding of Missouri charter facilities represent real vulnerabilities. The $13.6 million bond allocation addresses urgent physical plant needs, particularly the Armour campus gym and auditorium, while the 2,000-student growth target depends on sustained enrollment demand and the school's ability to recruit French-speaking educators from an internationally constrained pipeline. The school's technology stack is functional but lean, centered on PowerSchool and Google Workspace, with no evidence yet of AI tool adoption despite the superintendent's involvement in AI advisory work. For vendors and partners, the school's combination of high performance, active expansion, facility investment, and bilingual staffing needs presents multiple engagement opportunities across edtech, facilities, HR, and French-language education services.
