# The Global Orphan Project: CAIO strategic growth profile

**The Global Orphan Project (GO Project) is a fast-scaling, $32M faith-based social enterprise that has quietly built one of the most differentiated models in orphan care.** Founded in 2004 in Kansas City, Missouri, the organization has nearly doubled its revenue in three years by combining a proprietary technology platform (CarePortal), a fair-trade apparel brand (GOEX), and international family-strengthening programs — all routed through local churches rather than branded field operations. With **189 employees, 54,000+ volunteers**, a perfect Charity Navigator score, and program service revenue growing 5x since 2022, GO Project operates more like a mission-driven holding company than a traditional nonprofit.

---

## Financial trajectory shows accelerating momentum

GO Project's most recent Form 990 (FY2024, filed October 2025) reveals **$32.3 million in total revenue**, a striking **50.5% year-over-year increase** from $21.5M in FY2023. This caps a sustained growth arc: revenue was $16.4M in FY2021 and $18.7M in FY2022. Total expenses were $26.4M in FY2024, producing a **$5.9M operating surplus** and pushing net assets to **$17.7M**.

The revenue mix is evolving meaningfully. Contributions (donations and grants) remain dominant at **$26.4M (82%)**, but the fastest-growing line is program service revenue — primarily CarePortal software licensing fees — which surged from **$1.0M in FY2022 to $5.4M in FY2024**, a fivefold increase. GOEX apparel generates a modest $501K in net inventory sales. Government grants totaled $436K in FY2023. This diversification toward earned income is strategically significant; CarePortal's recurring SaaS-like revenue creates a more sustainable financial base than pure donation dependency.

## Three platforms power a distinctive enterprise model

GO Project's structure is unusual in the nonprofit world. It operates as an **"enterprise" backbone** supporting three distinct platforms, each with its own president and strategic mandate:

**CarePortal** is the flagship. Launched in 2015 in one Kansas county, this proprietary technology platform connects government child welfare caseworkers with local church response teams in real time. When a caseworker enters a family need — diapers, a bed, rent assistance — nearby churches see it and respond. The model has scaled to **2,800+ church response teams across 27+ states**, serving **125,000+ children** with **$40M+ mobilized** from community responders. CarePortal's strategic goal is to cover every major U.S. market by 2025 and expand globally by 2030. Its framing — "care-sharing as common as ride-sharing" — signals tech-sector ambitions. A UX product audit with design firm Crema is preparing the platform for international scaling.

**GOEX (GO Exchange)** is a fair-trade certified apparel company with production in Haiti and Kansas City, providing dignified, livable-wage employment to prevent poverty-driven family separation. While small in revenue terms ($501K net), it serves as a proof-of-concept social enterprise and a tangible donor engagement tool.

**Strong Family** is the international program arm, co-founded by Dr. Alison Barfoot and Rose Kanyunyuzi. It delivers culturally relevant family-strengthening training — financial literacy, job skills, spiritual development — through local churches in the developing world, supported by a mobile app and technology framework.

## Leadership, governance, and organizational scale

**Joe Knittig** has served as Enterprise CEO since at least 2023 (previously a trial lawyer with a J.D. from the University of Missouri). The leadership team reflects the multi-platform structure: **Jacob Barreth** (President and COO), **Adrien Lewis** (President of CarePortal), **Jessica Ray** (President of GOEX), **Bethany Bezanson** (CFO), and **Corey Vaudo** (Chief Data & Information Officer). Four international Executive Directors lead regional operations: GO Haiti, GO Africa, GO India, and GO MENA (Middle East & North Africa).

The nine-member board is chaired by co-founder **Mike Fox**, the Kansas City businessman who launched the organization in 2004 after encountering orphans in Southeast Asia. His wife **Beth Fox** serves as Vice President. Other board members include John Larimer (Secretary), Ed Barber (Treasurer), Alan Dietrich, Chris Levy, Jeffrey Murphy, Aaron Blake, and Bruce Kusmin. All board members serve without compensation. The board maintains conflict of interest, whistleblower, and document retention policies, and conducts annual CEO performance reviews.

The organization reported **189 employees** and **54,345 volunteers** on its FY2023 Form 990, up from approximately 124 employees in FY2021. This headcount growth tracks with the revenue expansion and CarePortal's geographic scaling. International staff under the four regional sister organizations may be additional.

## Geographic reach spans four continents and 27+ U.S. states

GO Project operates internationally through four sister implementing organizations serving approximately **11 countries**: GO Haiti (Haiti, Dominican Republic), GO Africa (Uganda, DRC, Ethiopia), GO India, and GO MENA (Middle East & North Africa). Domestically, CarePortal has expanded across **27+ states** from its Kansas City headquarters at 3161 Wyandotte Street. GOEX maintains production facilities in both Haiti and Kansas City.

The church-as-delivery-mechanism model is central. GO Project deliberately stays behind the scenes — it does not brand its field presence. Local churches are positioned as the "heroes," receiving real-time needs data and responding directly. This creates organic community ownership but also means GO Project's brand recognition may lag behind its actual footprint.

## Sector headwinds make GO Project's model especially relevant

The nonprofit orphan care sector faces significant structural pressures. **Donor retention has fallen for four consecutive years**, reaching just **42.9% overall in 2024** (down 2.6% year-over-year), with first-time donor retention cratering to **19.4%**. The sector is experiencing a "dollars up, donors down" concentration — fewer donors giving more, with micro-donors (under $100) declining 8.8%. The total number of 501(c)(3) organizations has grown **85% since 2000 to 1.5 million**, intensifying competition for philanthropic dollars.

Against this backdrop, GO Project's model has several structural advantages. Its CarePortal platform generates **recurring program service revenue** that is less vulnerable to donor fatigue. The Founders' Circle overhead model insulates program funding from administrative cost pressures. And the church mobilization approach — with 54,000+ volunteers — creates a distributed cost structure that traditional field-office models cannot match.

The orphan care sector itself is shifting strongly toward **family-based care and deinstitutionalization**, aligned with UNICEF guidelines and the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child. GO Project's emphasis on family preservation ("Keep Families Together") and prevention rather than institutional orphan care puts it squarely on the right side of this global trend. Key competitors include large players like **Compassion International** (~$1B+ revenue, child sponsorship), **World Vision** (~$1B+), and **Bethany Christian Services** (adoption and foster care), plus similarly positioned organizations like **Lifesong for Orphans** (which also uses a "100% to programs" model) and **Holt International**.

Industry fundraising cost benchmarks typically range from **$0.20–$0.35 per dollar raised**, with administrative overhead expectations of 15–25%. Monthly/recurring giving now represents **31% of online nonprofit revenue** and is growing 5% annually — a format well-suited to GO Project's church-based community model.

## Conclusion: key takeaways for the CAIO strategic growth report

GO Project's core strategic asset is **CarePortal's technology platform**, which is transitioning from a program tool to a scalable revenue engine. The 5x growth in program service revenue ($1M → $5.4M in two years) is the most important financial signal — it suggests a path toward sustainable earned income that most nonprofits of this size cannot replicate.

Several data points deserve particular attention for executive planning. The **FY2024 revenue jump of 50.5%** is dramatic and should be interrogated — is this a one-time large gift or sustainable trajectory? The FY2023 audit flagged a **"significant deficiency in internal controls"** (below material weakness but worth monitoring as the organization scales). Executive compensation **doubled from $568K to $1.2M** in FY2024, reflecting team expansion. And the 990 discloses **conflict of interest transactions** on Schedule L for both FY2023 and FY2024 — standard for founder-led organizations but worth reviewing.

The organization's differentiators — a tech platform with network effects, a social enterprise revenue stream, a separate overhead funding model, and a church-partnership delivery system operating across four continents — position it as one of the more innovative mid-size players in faith-based child welfare. The strategic question is whether CarePortal's domestic scaling and planned global expansion can maintain the growth trajectory while the underlying nonprofit fundraising environment continues to tighten.
