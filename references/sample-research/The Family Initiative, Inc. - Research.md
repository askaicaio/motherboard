# The Family Initiative: Strategic Intelligence Profile

**The Family Initiative, Inc. (TFI) is a $134 million, Kansas-headquartered child welfare nonprofit that has emerged as one of the largest Community-Based Care agencies in the United States.** Founded in 1965 as a rural day camp, TFI now operates across five states through six affiliate entities, employs approximately 1,600 staff, and serves over 8,900 children annually in foster care, adoption, case management, behavioral health, and residential treatment. The organization's **68.5% revenue surge in FY2024** — driven by a landmark contract to manage child welfare across the Dallas metro area — signals an aggressive growth trajectory that positions TFI at the intersection of government-contracted child welfare services and proprietary technology commercialization. This profile synthesizes findings from IRS 990 filings, the organization's website, ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer, LinkedIn, job postings, press releases, and industry data to support strategic AI growth analysis.

## Sixty years from day camp to national child welfare powerhouse

TFI traces its origins to **1965**, when Maxine Johnson and her family launched a rural day camp for youth in Admire, Kansas. The organization evolved through several inflection points: opening an emergency shelter in Emporia, obtaining its first child-placing agency license in **1990**, becoming a Kansas state subcontractor for foster care in **1996**, and achieving Council on Accreditation (COA) status in **1999**. The rebrand to TFI Family Services occurred around 2005, followed by geographic expansion into Nebraska and Oklahoma (2012) and the formation of Texas Family Initiative LLC in 2014.

The parent entity — The Family Initiative, Inc. (EIN: **84-1880993**) — was restructured in 2020 as a holding company for six affiliates:

| Affiliate | Jurisdiction | Focus |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| TFI Family Services, Inc. | Kansas, Nebraska, Missouri | Core foster care and case management |
| TFI Family Connections, LLC | Oklahoma | Therapeutic foster care |
| Texas Family Initiative, LLC | Texas | CBC contracts (2INgage, EMPOWER) |
| Pathway Family Services, LLC | Kansas, Texas | Residential treatment (PRTF, QRTP) |
| Mainstream Nonprofit Solutions, Inc. | Multi-state | Administrative services for TFI and external nonprofits |
| Foster Care Technologies, LLC | Multi-state | Proprietary child welfare software (ECAP) |

TFI's mission — **"Devoted to the Strength of Family"** — is operationalized through a vision to be "nationally recognized for excellence in service, education, and advocacy." The organization claims services across **nine states**, though primary operations concentrate in Kansas, Texas, Oklahoma, Nebraska, and Missouri. Headquarters functions split between **Topeka, KS** (217 SE 4th Street) and **Emporia, KS** (original home, financial operations).

## Programs span the full child welfare continuum

TFI delivers services across virtually every segment of the child welfare spectrum, organized into eight major categories:

**Foster care and kinship care** form the operational core, including traditional foster care placement and licensing across all five states, kinship care programs, professional foster care for higher-needs youth (Texas), therapeutic foster care (Oklahoma), and treatment foster care (Texas). The organization maintains a network of **600+ licensed foster parents** and operates dedicated recruitment infrastructure through tfirecruitment.org.

**Case management** encompasses out-of-home case management in Kansas (Areas 4 and 8 covering Southeast and South-Central Kansas), aftercare services, special response teams, relative/kinship teams, and the massive Community-Based Care contracts in Texas. The **2INgage** program (partnership with New Horizons Inc.) serves as Single Source Continuum Contractor for Texas DFPS Region 2, while **EMPOWER** covers Region 3E (Metroplex East) across nine Dallas-area counties — Collin, Dallas, Ellis, Fannin, Grayson, Hunt, Kaufman, Navarro, and Rockwall — serving **2,000+ children daily**.

**Clinical and behavioral health services** include counseling, community clinical services, and Texas Family First programming aligned with FFPSA requirements. **Prevention and family preservation** programs — including the FFPSA-funded Grow Nurturing Families initiative using Parent-Child Interaction Therapy — represent TFI's investment in the sector's strategic shift from placement to prevention. **Residential treatment** through Pathway Family Services operates psychiatric residential treatment facilities (PRTF) in Topeka and the Blue Skies facility in Wichita Falls, TX (opened Spring 2023), using evidence-based interventions including TBRI, PBIS, and TF-CBT.

**Independent living programs** (TRAIL, Chafee Program, Regional Youth Advisory Council) serve aging-out youth. **Visitation exchange centers** operate in Lawrence and Topeka, Kansas. **Training and education** encompasses TIPS-MAPP foster parent training, Solution-Based Casework, and the TFI Leadership Institute for internal staff development.

## Technology infrastructure reveals proprietary competitive advantage

TFI's most strategically significant asset may be **Foster Care Technologies (FCT)**, its proprietary software subsidiary. FCT's flagship product, **ECAP (Every Child a Priority)**, is an algorithm-driven placement matching tool that uses statistical models to match children with foster families based on evidence-based compatibility factors. University of Kansas School of Social Welfare researchers validated ECAP's impact: a **23% increase in placement stability** and a **two-month decrease in average time to permanency**. ECAP integrates with other agencies' case management systems and is commercially licensed to external public and private child welfare organizations.

The **Foster Family Portal** — a desktop and mobile application (iOS/Android) launched in September 2020 — provides foster parents with licensing and renewal document tracking, current placement information, ECAP-powered match suggestions, and notification functionality. FCT also offers **Placement Process Consulting** services and developed the **APLI (Appropriate Placement Level Indicator)** child assessment tool. Key technology leaders include Paul Epp (VP, Foster Care Technologies), Kyle Manahan (Senior Data & Business Analyst, 30+ years in software development, built TFI's original home-grown applications), and Beverly Corkill (VP of Data and Report Validation).

TFI's broader IT infrastructure runs on a **Microsoft 365 ecosystem** with an AI chatbot in Microsoft Teams under development. The organization is pursuing cloud-based faxing, corporate Wi-Fi modernization, and a "paperless" initiative. The website runs on WordPress with state-specific subdomains. Critically, TFI appears to have built **home-grown software rather than relying on commercial case management platforms** like ETO, Apricot, or Penelope — a notable differentiator suggesting deep internal technical capability. The organization uses **Predictive Index** for HR hiring assessments and likely Paylocity for payroll processing.

TFI's revenue depends almost entirely on government contracts. Active state contracts include foster care case management in Kansas Areas 4 and 8 (Kansas DCF), foster care and adoption services in Kansas Regions 1 and 3, family preservation in western Kansas and Region 3, the 2INgage CBC contract in Texas DFPS Region 2, and the EMPOWER CBC contract in Texas DFPS Region 3E. ## The child welfare sector faces compounding structural pressures

TFI operates in a sector experiencing unprecedented turbulence. **Workforce crisis** dominates: caseworker turnover averages **30% nationally**, with some states exceeding 40%. The median caseworker stays just **1.8 years**, and each departure costs an estimated **$54,000**. Child, family, and school social workers earn a median of just **$53,940** annually, creating persistent recruitment challenges against private-sector alternatives. The NSCAW III Workforce Study (2025) found that 74.9% of supervisors identify burnout as the top turnover driver.

The **Family First Prevention Services Act (FFPSA)** continues reshaping the sector's funding architecture. As of September 2024, **42 states plus D.C. and 4 tribes** have approved Title IV-E prevention plans, redirecting federal dollars from placement toward evidence-based prevention services. This structural shift benefits organizations like TFI that offer prevention programming alongside traditional placement services. Combined federal, state, and local child welfare spending exceeds **$34 billion annually**, with federal programs contributing approximately $11 billion.

The foster care population has declined from a 2018 peak of **437,000 to approximately 329,000 in FY2024**, driven by prevention emphasis and kinship care expansion (now **39% of all placements**). However, **federal uncertainty looms large in 2025–2026**: HHS staff cuts reduced Administration for Children and Families personnel by 45%, Social Services Block Grant administration ($1.7B annually) lost all distributing staff, and approximately $10 billion in child welfare-related funds were frozen for certain states. Technology adoption remains slow — most agencies run legacy systems, and while AI interest is growing, bias concerns and data quality issues constrain adoption. The transition from SACWIS to CCWIS-compliant systems (**45 states declared**) represents a multi-year, multi-million-dollar modernization wave.

## Strategic positioning and growth vectors

TFI's strategic trajectory is defined by several visible growth vectors. The **Community-Based Care model** is the primary expansion engine — EMPOWER's March 2024 launch made TFI one of the largest CBC agencies nationally, and the organization now holds four CBC foster care contracts plus two family preservation contracts. TFI's founding membership in the **National Center on Community-Based Child Welfare** positions it to influence sector policy and potentially secure additional CBC contracts as more states explore this model.

**Technology commercialization** through Foster Care Technologies represents a differentiated growth lever. ECAP's validated placement-matching algorithm and the Foster Family Portal create recurring licensing revenue opportunities as agencies modernize under CCWIS mandates. The internal development of an **AI chatbot in Microsoft Teams** and the presence of senior technology leaders (VP of IT, VP of Software, Senior Data Analyst) at top-compensation levels suggest continued investment in technology capabilities.

**Service line expansion** continues through residential treatment (Blue Skies facility, PFS Health & Wellness Center), FFPSA-aligned prevention programs (Grow Nurturing Families), and therapeutic foster care growth in Oklahoma. TFI's COA accreditation — maintained since 1999 and recently renewed — covers child and family development, family foster care and kinship care, residential treatment, mental health/substance use services, supervised visitation, and youth independent living. Professional affiliations span the Alliance for Strong Families and Communities, Children's Alliance of Kansas, Texas Alliance of Child & Family Services, and the Association for Children's Residential Centers.

## Conclusion

TFI presents as a high-growth, government-contract-dependent nonprofit with genuine technology differentiation through Foster Care Technologies and ECAP. Three dynamics warrant close attention for AI growth strategy: **first**, the EMPOWER contract's full annualization will likely push FY2025 revenue past $155M, creating scale that demands more sophisticated data infrastructure; **second**, TFI's home-grown software tradition and existing algorithmic placement tools (ECAP) provide an unusually strong internal foundation for AI adoption compared to peers reliant on commercial platforms; **third**, the organization's razor-thin margins (4.7% in the best year, net losses in prior years) mean any AI investment must deliver measurable cost reduction or contract-winning capability. The convergence of federal CCWIS modernization mandates, FFPSA evidence-based practice requirements, and a 30% sector turnover rate creates an environment where AI-powered case management, predictive analytics, and workflow automation could be transformational — and TFI's proprietary technology subsidiary positions it to not only adopt but potentially commercialize these solutions.
