# EPC Real Estate Group: strategic growth intelligence report

EPC Real Estate Group is a privately held, vertically integrated real estate developer and property manager headquartered in Overland Park, Kansas, with an estimated **$10–15 million in annual operating revenue** and approximately **$1.5 billion in cumulative capital deployed** across 10,000+ residential units and 1.15 million square feet of commercial and industrial space. Originally founded as ePartment Communities in 1999–2001 by Terence P. O'Leary and rebranded under current President and CEO Michael J. McKeen in 2014, the firm has evolved from a technology-focused apartment concept into a diversified development platform spanning luxury multifamily, mixed-use, senior housing, commercial, industrial, and — most recently — hospitality. This report synthesizes findings from the company's website, business data aggregators, local media, public records, and industry databases to present a comprehensive picture of EPC's operations, financials, technology stack, competitive position, and strategic trajectory.

---

## From wireless apartments to $1.5 billion in deployed capital

EPC Real Estate Group traces its origins to 1999, when Terence P. O'Leary conceptualized ePartment Communities around a then-novel idea: the first wireless apartment community, developed in 2001. The company operated under that name for over a decade before Michael J. McKeen — a construction industry veteran from Turner Construction and Briarcliff Development Company — joined in 2014 and led a comprehensive restructuring and rebrand to EPC Real Estate Group (the "EPC" acronym preserving the original ePartment Communities identity).

Under McKeen's leadership, the firm has scaled dramatically. McKeen personally has been involved in **7,700+ multifamily and hotel units**, **900,000 square feet of commercial space**, and has secured over **$1.8 billion in capital**. The company's mission — "reshaping the industry by creating innovative lifestyle properties" — reflects a brand-forward, amenity-rich approach that distinguishes it from commodity-oriented developers. The firm was inducted into the Overland Park Chamber of Commerce **Business Hall of Honor in 2024**, and McKeen has been recognized by Ingram's Magazine as both "40 Under Forty" and "20 in Their Twenties."

**Ownership structure** is private and partnership-based. EPC Real Estate Group LLC is owned and operated by McKeen (President, CEO & Owner), O'Leary (Chairman & Founding Partner), and Brendon O'Leary (Executive Vice President & Partner, likely a family relation to Terence). No private equity backing or public listing exists. The only external capital identified is two SBA PPP loans totaling approximately $350,000–$650,000 received during the pandemic. The company maintains an active **Investor Portal** for deal-level capital partners, indicating a syndication or joint-venture model for project financing.

## Five integrated business lines anchor the platform

EPC operates as a vertically integrated platform across five core service lines, with development and property management serving as the primary revenue engines:

**Development** is the flagship business. EPC develops ground-up Class A multifamily, mixed-use, commercial, senior housing, and master-planned communities. The firm handles the full lifecycle: site selection, entitlements, design team assembly, construction oversight, lease-up, and stabilization. Key metrics include 10,000+ units delivered or managed, 550,000+ SF commercial, and 600,000+ SF industrial. The expansion into **hospitality** (389 hotel keys in pipeline, including the Unscripted hotel brand in Wichita) represents a significant new vertical.

**Property management** operates as a full-service third-party platform managing multifamily, mixed-use, HUD-financed, office, retail, and senior housing assets. The Apartments.com portfolio shows 16 communities and 2,976 units across eight Kansas and Missouri cities. This business generates recurring fee income and provides EPC with operational intelligence that informs development decisions.

**Acquisitions** focus on value-add opportunities and strategic land assembly. Notable examples include the competitive RFP win for 7.5 acres of blighted land in Wichita's Delano District (2018) and the adaptive reuse of five historic manufacturing buildings for West Bottoms Flats (265 apartments from 341,000 SF of industrial space).

**Investment management** encompasses capital deployment, investor relations, and portfolio oversight, supported by a dedicated investor portal. The firm has completed **10+ investment exits**, suggesting a proven track record of delivering returns to capital partners.

**Strategic partnerships** — particularly public-private partnerships — represent a distinctive competency. EPC has secured significant public incentives including **$11.3 million in TIF financing** for the Wichita Ballpark District and Port KC incentives for the 74 Broadway project. Partners include DLR Group, Brinkmann Constructors, Ryan Companies, Dream Hotel Group, and the joint venture 3G Development LLC (formed with former Copaken Brooks principals).

## Technology stack centers on Entrata and EOS, with PropTech upside

EPC's confirmed technology infrastructure is functional but not cutting-edge, representing a clear opportunity area for strategic investment:

**Property management software**: **Entrata** serves as the primary platform (confirmed by ZoomInfo technology detection), handling leasing, resident management, accounting, marketing, and maintenance workflows across the portfolio. Entrata is a well-regarded mid-market PMS solution, though it lacks some of the enterprise-grade analytics capabilities of competitors like Yardi Voyager or RealPage.

**Business operating system**: The firm runs on **EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System)**, prominently displayed on the website footer. EOS provides a structured framework for vision-setting, traction, and team health — common among growth-stage private companies in the $5–50M revenue range.

**Productivity and communications**: **Microsoft 365** powers office productivity. **Sched** is used for event scheduling/management.

**Web and marketing technology**: WordPress CMS, Cloudflare CDN, Google Tag Manager, Issuu (digital brochures), and active social media presence across Instagram, LinkedIn, and Facebook.

**Investor relations**: A custom **Investor Portal** on the website facilitates LP/investor communications and reporting.

Notably absent from the confirmed stack are enterprise CRM tools (Salesforce, HubSpot), dedicated project management software (Procore, Buildertrend), advanced analytics platforms, or AI-enabled tools. Job postings on Indeed and Glassdoor focus on property management and maintenance roles rather than technology positions, further suggesting a lean technology operation. The company does emphasize **smart technology integration** in newer projects like The OsLo, indicating awareness of PropTech trends at the asset level even if corporate technology adoption lags.

## Real estate headwinds create both risk and opportunity for EPC's model

The macro environment as of early 2026 presents a complex landscape for a Midwest-focused multifamily developer:

**Interest rates remain elevated but are easing.** Thirty-year mortgage rates are projected to average **6.2–6.3% in 2026**, down from ~6.6% in 2025, with the potential to breach 6% by year-end. For EPC, lower rates improve project economics for development financing and support asset valuations at exit. The **$539 billion in commercial mortgages maturing in 2026** creates both refinancing risk and acquisition opportunity.

**Multifamily fundamentals are strengthening.** National apartment vacancy declined to **4.4%** (from 6.9% in late 2024), with net absorption more than doubling year-over-year to 82,500 units in Q1 2025. Rents are expected to rise **2–3% in 2026**. Critically for EPC, **Midwest markets are outperforming** the oversupplied Sun Belt — a structural advantage for their Kansas City-centric portfolio.

**PropTech investment is surging.** VC firms invested **$16.7 billion in proptech in 2025** (up 67.9% YoY), with AI-centered companies growing at **42% annualized**. EliseAI reached a $2.2 billion valuation providing AI leasing agents to major landlords. EPC's current technology stack suggests significant room to capture efficiency gains through AI-enabled leasing, predictive maintenance, and tenant management.

**Regulatory tailwinds from tax policy.** The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (enacted July 2025) permanently restored **100% bonus depreciation** through 2030 and made **Opportunity Zones permanent** with enhanced benefits starting 2027. For a developer that structures deals with investor capital and public incentives, these provisions materially improve after-tax returns and capital attraction.

**The housing deficit persists.** The U.S. housing shortage is estimated at **4.7 million units** (Zillow), and home prices have surged 50% since 2020 while incomes rose only 29%. This structural undersupply supports multifamily demand, particularly for EPC's luxury-lifestyle product that appeals to renters-by-choice in high-cost ownership markets.

## Mid-market positioning between local shops and national platforms

EPC occupies a distinct niche in the Kansas City development market — larger and more sophisticated than boutique local developers, but more nimble and community-embedded than national platforms. The competitive landscape includes:

**Larger-scale competitors**: **NorthPoint Development** (America's #1 industrial developer, expanding into multifamily), **Block Real Estate Services** (deep KC legacy, $350M Galleria 115 master plan), and **Copaken Brooks** (16M+ SF developed, longest-standing KC CRE firm). These firms operate at significantly greater scale but lack EPC's lifestyle-brand positioning.

**Regional peers**: **Milhaus** (Indianapolis-based, ~2,000 KC units), **Hunt Midwest** (Lamar Hunt family, diversified), and **Lux Living** (St. Louis, expanding into KC). These compete directly for Class A multifamily sites and tenants.

**National platforms**: **Ryan Companies**, **The Wolff Company**, **Davis Development**, and **Flaherty & Collins** all have KC-area operations but treat the market as one of many, lacking EPC's concentrated local expertise.

EPC differentiates through four mechanisms: a **lifestyle-brand approach** emphasizing wellness, smart technology, and resort-style amenities ("EPCLiving"); **public-private partnership expertise** that unlocks TIF financing and municipal incentives; **vertical integration** across development, management, and investment; and **deep community embeddedness** in the KC metro that provides informational advantages in site selection and entitlements.

## Conclusion: a growth-stage platform with scaling potential and clear investment thesis

EPC Real Estate Group has built a credible, vertically integrated development platform on the strength of $1.5 billion in deployed capital, a distinctive lifestyle brand, and deep Kansas City market knowledge. The firm's strategic trajectory — expansion into hospitality, industrial, senior housing, and new geographies (Salt Lake City, Texas) — signals ambition beyond its current mid-market scale.

Three factors will likely determine EPC's growth ceiling. First, **capital access**: the firm's syndication model and investor portal suggest reliance on deal-by-deal capital raises; a programmatic equity partnership or credit facility could dramatically accelerate deployment. Second, **technology modernization**: the current Entrata-plus-EOS stack is adequate but underweights analytics, AI-enabled operations, and CRM — areas where competitors with institutional backing are investing heavily. Third, **geographic expansion execution**: the Salt Lake City and Texas footholds are early-stage, and replicating KC-caliber community relationships and public incentive expertise in new markets is the classic scaling challenge for regionally dominant developers.

The macro environment is largely supportive: Midwest multifamily fundamentals are strong, bonus depreciation is restored through 2030, the housing deficit persists, and interest rates are trending downward. EPC's positioning in a market that economists increasingly view as a beneficiary of the Sun Belt-to-Midwest rotation adds a structural tailwind. For a strategic growth engagement, the highest-impact levers are likely capital structure optimization, technology-enabled operating margin improvement, and disciplined geographic expansion — building on a foundation that has already demonstrated the ability to develop, manage, and exit at meaningful scale.
