# The J Kansas City: AI Strategic ROI Fact Base

**The Jewish Community Center of Greater Kansas City ("The J") is a $19M-revenue, 110-year-old nonprofit community center** serving approximately **14,000 members** across fitness, aquatics, early childhood education, camping, performing arts, and senior services in Overland Park, Kansas. With nearly half its expenses concentrated in labor costs and growing earned revenue now exceeding donations, The J presents a classic AI opportunity profile: a high-touch, operationally complex community services organization with significant workforce, scheduling, and member engagement infrastructure ripe for augmentation.

---

## Mission, programs, and what The J actually does

**Legal name:** Jewish Community Center of Greater Kansas City | **EIN:** 44-0545992 | **Founded:** 1914 | **Tax-exempt since:** March 1947

**Mission:** *"To enrich our diverse community by cultivating an inclusive environment built upon Jewish values, heritage, and culture. We offer programs of excellence that enhance wellness, meaning and joy from generation to generation."*

The J is open to all faiths and backgrounds. It operates as a full-spectrum community center — not merely a fitness facility — with programming spanning the full lifecycle from infants to seniors. Its core program areas include:

- **Fitness & Wellness:** 80+ weekly group exercise classes, TechnoGym-equipped fitness floor, Meshugge CrossFit, Pilates Reformer studio, hot yoga studio, personal training, indoor track, sauna/steam room, and a dedicated Ben Rubin Parkinson's Wellness Program (in partnership with KC Ballet)
- **Aquatics:** Three swimming pools (indoor lap pool, outdoor Olympic-sized pool with slides/splash pad), swim lessons, Masters swim, competitive swim club, lifeguard certification, and a summer swim team
- **Early Childhood Education:** NAEYC-accredited Child Development Center operating at two sites (main campus for ages 1–5; B'nai Jehudah satellite for infants 6 weeks–2 years). A new infant care classroom launched in March 2024 with a second added in June 2024, creating 18 infant slots and 8+ new FTE positions
- **Youth & Camps:** ACA-accredited J Camp (day camp ages 2–13, **record 863 campers in 2024**), after-school care (Kids Konnection, K–6th), specialty camps (STEM, culinary, theatre, dance, art), and SafeSitter certification (280 graduates)
- **Performing Arts:** The Lewis & Shirley White Theatre, a **500-seat venue** now in its 21st season, sold **20,905 tickets in 2024** across a 9-show season including co-productions with KC Rep and Black Repertory Theatre of KC
- **Sports & Recreation:** Adult and youth leagues in basketball, soccer, softball, tennis, racquetball, pickleball, volleyball, and flag football
- **Senior Services (Heritage Center):** Five-day-a-week programming for adults 55+, serving **700+ unduplicated older adults annually**, including kosher meals, transportation services, fitness, education, and day trips
- **Jewish Life & Culture:** MeltonKC adult learning, Israeli Emissary program, KC Jewish Film Series, holiday programming, Acclaimed Author Series, and the Miller Introduction to Judaism Program
- **Signature Fundraiser:** KC SuperStar, an "American Idol"-style high school singing competition now in its 16th year, generates **$1M+ annually** and funds **$540K–$550K in financial assistance** plus $22K+ in scholarships

## One main campus, one satellite, and a recent 32,000 sq ft expansion

**Main Campus:** 5801 W. 115th Street, Overland Park, KS 66211 — situated on the Jewish Community Campus, which also houses the Hyman Brand Hebrew Academy and other Jewish organizations. The campus is described as "one of the largest community centers in the city in terms of sheer size."

**Satellite Location:** B'nai Jehudah CDC at The Temple, 12320 Nall Avenue, Overland Park — infant/toddler care for ages 6 weeks to 2 years.

**Major Facility Expansion Completed:** A **32,000 sq ft Family Recreation Complex** was recently completed, adding a second gymnasium, indoor turf field/stadium, hot yoga studio, dance studios, ceramics/art studios, expanded group exercise space, larger childcare area, enhanced-security entrance, and additional parking. The capital campaign originally targeted $10M ($8M construction, $2M endowment) and received a notable $1.8M anchor gift from St. Louis philanthropist Michael Staenberg.

Additional recent capital improvements include the **Merna & Sylvan Siegler Family Pavilion**, the **Reat's Place – Corporon Family Amphitheater** (both opened 2024), $1M in board-approved capital projects, after-school room renovations, and ongoing facility maintenance.

Operating hours are extensive: Mon–Thu 5 AM–10 PM, Fri 5 AM–6 PM, Sat–Sun 7 AM–8 PM.

## Two major developments define the current strategic moment

**Hosting the 2026 JCC Maccabi Games (August 2–7, 2026)** is a marquee opportunity. Approximately 1,000 Jewish teen athletes will descend on Kansas City — the first time KC has hosted since 1997. Bob Hennecke, Director of Sports & Recreation, leads local operations. This event will stress-test the organization's logistics, volunteer management, hospitality, and communications infrastructure.

**The Royals stadium threat is a potentially existential issue.** The Kansas City Royals are evaluating the **Aspiria campus** (200 acres directly adjacent to The J) as a potential site for a new baseball stadium. In December 2025, the **JCC Association of North America** sent a formal letter to Kansas Governor Laura Kelly calling the prospect "devastating" and urging an alternative location. Concerns include multi-year construction disruption, traffic congestion from 80+ home games annually, parking displacement, and security risks. One member estimated relocation costs at **"north of eight figures."** As of early 2026, The J has declined to comment publicly. This issue has significant implications for any long-term capital or technology investment planning.

## Technology stack is functional but fragmented, with no AI footprint

The J operates a patchwork of purpose-built tools with **no evidence of any AI adoption, digital transformation strategy, or public technology roadmap**:

| System | Use Case |
|---|---|
| thejkc.org (CMS-based, HTML5/jQuery/Cloudflare) | Member portal, online class reservations, program enrollment |
| Remini App | CDC parent-teacher communication (photos, daily reports) |
| CampDoc (DocNetwork) | Camp enrollment, registration, payment processing |
| QuickScores | Sports league scheduling and standings |
| jccbaseball.com | Youth sports registration |
| Facebook Pixel | Website analytics/retargeting |

The organization has no mobile app, no unified CRM visible in public materials, and no apparent marketing automation beyond basic email newsletters. The member portal requires login and supports class reservations and program enrollment. Membership pricing is handled offline, suggesting limited e-commerce integration.

This fragmented technology landscape — with separate systems for childcare, camps, sports, fitness, and general member management — is typical of community centers this size and represents a clear AI opportunity area across scheduling optimization, member engagement personalization, staff communication, and operational efficiency.

## Industry headwinds create urgency for operational efficiency

The JCC sector collectively represents **$1.6 billion in annual operating budgets** across ~150 JCCs nationally, employing **38,000 people** and serving **1.5 million+ people weekly**. But the industry faces compounding pressures that make AI-driven efficiency gains particularly valuable:

**Staffing remains the dominant challenge.** In 2025, **58.6% of nonprofit community center respondents** cited staffing as a top concern. The J's own payroll grew 25.5% in two years — faster than revenue growth — creating margin pressure. Indeed reviews reference post-COVID turnover in middle management and wages not keeping pace with inflation.

**Facility costs are relentless.** 55% of JCC/Y respondents cited equipment and facility maintenance as a top concern. The J just completed a $10M+ expansion and approved another $1M in capital projects, with ongoing maintenance needs across three pools, multiple gyms, a 500-seat theatre, and extended operating hours (17 hours/day on weekdays).

**Competition from for-profit gyms squeezes the fitness value proposition.** JCC memberships typically run **$55–$82+/month**, while Planet Fitness offers $15/month and boutique studios offer specialized experiences. JCCs must compete on community value, breadth of programming, and mission — not price.

**Fundraising pressure is constant.** While The J's contribution revenue held relatively stable at ~$9.2M, the share of total revenue from donations declined from 55.6% to 48.6% over three years as the organization increasingly relies on earned income.

**The Kansas City Jewish community is aging.** The median age of Jewish adults in Greater KC is **60** (vs. 49 nationally), with only 22% of households including children — below the national average. This demographic skew toward older adults has implications for program demand, membership longevity, and digital adoption patterns.

The JCC Association of North America is responding with a major restructuring under new President/CEO Barak Hermann (July 2024), including three new "Centers of Excellence" and a planned **40% staff increase** at the national level, signaling field-wide recognition that JCCs need modernized infrastructure and support.

## Conclusion: key inputs for the AI ROI model

The J Kansas City is a **financially stable, operationally complex, labor-intensive community organization** generating $18.9M in revenue with nearly half flowing to compensation. Three data points frame the AI opportunity most sharply: **$8.9M in annual labor costs** (the primary lever for efficiency gains), **14,000 members across dozens of program areas** (the primary lever for engagement and retention optimization), and **a fragmented technology stack with zero AI adoption** (suggesting both low-hanging fruit and organizational readiness work ahead).

The organization's near-term strategic priorities — hosting the 2026 Maccabi Games, navigating the Royals stadium uncertainty, sustaining post-expansion momentum, and managing rising labor costs against narrowing surplus margins ($900K in FY 2024, down from $1.9M in FY 2022) — all create contexts where AI-driven efficiency, predictive analytics, and automation could deliver measurable ROI. The absence of any public AI or digital transformation posture suggests this would be a greenfield engagement with significant upside potential but requiring change management investment.
