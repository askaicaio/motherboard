# Sosland Publishing: AI strategic growth briefing

**Sosland Publishing is a 103-year-old, family-owned B2B trade publisher generating an estimated $20–32M in annual revenue with ~100–120 employees, serving the grain, baking, meat, dairy, and pet food industries from Kansas City.** The company operates nine core publications reaching 470,000+ registered readers and has demonstrated consistent organic growth — including double-digit digital revenue gains across multiple titles in 2024. But Sosland sits at a critical inflection: no niche food/ag trade publisher in its peer set has deployed meaningful AI capabilities, creating both an existential risk and a first-mover opportunity in a segment where large competitors like RELX and Informa are investing billions in AI-powered data products.

## A classic B2B media model with digital momentum building

Sosland operates a traditional B2B media business with five revenue streams, **advertising being the dominant source**, supplemented by events, subscriptions, data products, and digital services. No official revenue breakdown exists, but the company's own reporting reveals the trajectory.

**Advertising** drives the business across both print and digital. Digital sales are growing fastest: *Milling & Baking News* saw a **22% jump in digital sales** in 2024; *Baking & Snack* grew sales 9%; *Food Business News* revenue rose 5%; and the three newest titles (*Pet Food Processing*, *Supermarket Perimeter*, *Dairy Processing*) each grew revenues **20%+** in 2023. COO Meyer Sosland has confirmed that "all our titles are now in the webinar, e-newsletter, eblast, run of site and podcast business."

**Events revenue** is expanding as Sosland launches new properties. The events portfolio has grown from one signature seminar to five distinct properties in seven years. **Subscriptions** contribute revenue through paid access to premium content, particularly *Milling & Baking News* and commodity data products. **Data and reference products** — directories, redbooks, buyer's guides — represent a nascent but strategically important stream that could evolve into higher-value digital offerings.

For financial benchmarking, **typical EBITDA margins for small/mid-size B2B trade publishers range from 10–20%**, with events-heavy portfolios pushing toward 25–35% and data/analytics businesses achieving 30–40%. **SG&A ratios** in publishing and information services typically run **15–22% of revenue**. Applied to Sosland's estimated revenue, this suggests EBITDA in the **$2–6M range** — consistent with valuations of **4–8x EBITDA** that small B2B media companies command in M&A markets.

## Recent momentum: record events, new launches, and digital acceleration

The 2022–2025 period has been one of the most active in the company's century-long history. In **2022**, Sosland celebrated its centennial and executed a major website redesign across all properties, migrating paid subscriptions to the Omeda platform. **2023** saw the three newest titles each achieve 20%+ revenue growth and the Purchasing Seminar draw 33% more attendees. **2024** brought the most consequential developments: the launch of the **Pet Food Processing Exchange** event, record Purchasing Seminar turnout, the IBIE 2025 media partnership, and double-digit digital growth across the portfolio. For **2025**, *MEAT+POULTRY* marks its 70th anniversary, *Food Business News* its 20th, and the company appears to have relocated to new offices at 2345 Grand Blvd.

The growth pattern is telling: Sosland has invested in **new vertical launches** (Pet Food Processing in 2018, Dairy Processing in 2021, Food Entrepreneur in 2020) and **new events** rather than acquisitions or technology platforms. This organic strategy has produced steady growth but leaves significant digital transformation headroom.

## The industry is bifurcating — and AI is the dividing line

The B2B media industry is undergoing a structural split. **Data and analytics businesses** (exemplified by RELX at **34.8% operating margins** on £9.6B revenue) are thriving. **Events-heavy portfolios** generate 25–35% margins with strong post-COVID recovery. **Traditional trade magazine businesses** face structural decline: print ad revenue is falling at a **-13.5% CAGR** through 2029, and overall trade magazine revenue carries a **-2.6% CAGR**. The total U.S. B2B media market is roughly **$93–98 billion**, but the trade magazine segment — where Sosland primarily operates — represents only ~6% and is shrinking.

The AI investment gap between large and small publishers is stark. **RELX** spends **$1.9 billion annually on technology**, employs 12,000+ technologists, and has launched **17 commercially available generative AI products** including Lexis+ AI, Scopus AI, and ClinicalKey AI. **Informa** has secured **$75M+ in AI data licensing deals** and is building an enterprise AI platform with "specialized expert agents" based on proprietary content. Meanwhile, **no niche food/ag trade publisher in Sosland's peer set has announced significant AI initiatives**. This creates a window.

Key industry benchmarks relevant to Sosland's strategic planning:

- **B2B digital ad spending**: $18.3B in 2024 (growing ~15% YoY)
- **71% of B2B marketers** now use generative AI weekly
- **56% of B2B media platforms** have integrated AI-driven tools by 2025
- **Digital subscription revenue** in B2B publishing grew **27%** recently
- **M&A multiples**: Media sector averages ~11.5x EV/EBITDA; small B2B media companies trade at 4–8x

## Competitive landscape reveals both moats and gaps

Sosland competes in a fragmented niche. Its closest competitors are a mix of family-owned specialists, PE-backed consolidators, and divisions of global media conglomerates.

| Competitor | Overlap with Sosland | Size/Ownership | Digital/AI Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Watt Global Media** | Pet food, feed, poultry | Family-owned, 100+ yrs | Traditional; no AI initiatives |
| **BNP Media** (food titles) | Snack, dairy, meat processing | Private, multi-vertical | Moved food titles to digital-only (2020); BNP Engage platform |
| **William Reed** (FoodNavigator) | Bakery, dairy, food mfg | Independent, UK; 5M monthly users | Most digitally advanced food media company globally |

| **Farm Journal Media** | Grain, dairy, ag | Management-owned, Philadelphia | **4.3M buyer profiles** in proprietary data engine; most data-forward mid-size ag publisher |
| **Endeavor/Food Processing** | Food/beverage manufacturing | PE-backed (80+ brands) | Scale digital infrastructure across 9M+ decision-makers |

**Sosland's moat is deep but narrow.** No competitor matches its coverage of the full grain-to-shelf food chain. *Milling & Baking News* has no true peer. The Purchasing Seminar occupies a unique position in commodity market events. The company's century of editorial authority and family-ownership stability provide credibility that PE-backed competitors cannot easily replicate.

**Where competitors lead:** William Reed's FoodNavigator draws **5M monthly users globally** — dwarfing Sosland's digital footprint. Urner Barry/Mintec has built a **SaaS commodity pricing platform** with 4,100+ proprietary prices that could erode the value of Sosland's Purchasing Seminar and commodity content. Farm Journal Media's **4.3M-profile behavioral data engine** represents the kind of first-party data asset that commands premium valuations and enables AI applications. The consolidation of Endeavor Business Media (80+ brands) and Informa's continued acquisition spree are creating scale advantages in digital infrastructure that smaller publishers cannot match organically.

## The AI opportunity: first-mover advantage in a lagging segment

The strategic case for AI investment at Sosland rests on three pillars. First, **no direct competitor has moved** — the window for first-mover advantage in food/ag trade publishing AI is open. Second, Sosland possesses **103 years of proprietary content, commodity data, and audience intelligence** that could fuel high-value AI products. Third, the company's **organic growth discipline and family ownership** allow for long-horizon investment without PE-driven short-termism.

Concrete AI opportunities map to Sosland's existing assets. The company's commodity content (MarketFocus, Sweetener Report, Purchasing Seminar data) could power **AI-driven pricing intelligence tools** — following the Urner Barry/Mintec playbook. Its 470,000+ registered audience generates behavioral data that could fuel **AI-powered audience segmentation and intent-based advertising products** — following the Farm Journal model. Editorial content across nine titles could be leveraged for **AI-generated market briefs, automated content repurposing, and personalized newsletter delivery**. And directory/reference products (Baking & Snack Directory, RED BOOK, Grain & Milling Annual) are natural candidates for **AI-searchable databases and recommendation engines**.

The risk of inaction is equally clear. As RELX and Informa demonstrate, the B2B media companies that transform proprietary content into AI-powered decision tools command **2–3x the margins** of traditional publishers. The gap between AI leaders and laggards in B2B media is widening — and for a company of Sosland's size, the cost of falling behind may be irreversible.

---

## Conclusion

Sosland Publishing is a profitable, growing, well-managed niche publisher with genuine competitive moats — domain authority, editorial credibility, vertical breadth, and audience loyalty built over a century. Its recent trajectory (new title launches, record event attendance, double-digit digital gains) demonstrates operational vitality. But the company's strategic position is defined by an emerging tension: **its competitive advantages are analog assets in an increasingly AI-driven industry**. The $20–32M revenue base, ~100–120 person team, and family-ownership structure mean Sosland cannot match the technology spending of RELX ($1.9B) or Informa ($75M+ in AI licensing alone). What it can do is leverage its uniquely deep, proprietary content and audience data — assets that large competitors cannot replicate — to build targeted AI capabilities that enhance editorial productivity, create new data products, and deepen audience engagement. The competitive window is open today. Among niche food/ag trade publishers, no one has moved first. That is both the opportunity and the urgency.
